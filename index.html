    What is Financial Aid?

    Financial Aid is how you will get money from the government and from the college you are attending to help pay for your tuition.


    Requirement:

  • If you are a legal resident of NY and your family makes less than $125 thousand your tuition (not including room and board if you live on campus) to a CUNY or SUNY school will be completely covered by financial aid.
  • You will need to fill out 2 Financial Aid applications (FAFSA & TAP).

    • FAFSA

    FAFSA LINK
  • An FSA ID for yourself.
  • An FSA ID for your parent, as long as they have a SSN.
  • You will need to know your parents Social Security number, birthdate, and the date that they were married, divorced, or separated if applicable.

  • To have completed your FAFSA application.
  • You will need to know your parents Social Security number, birthdate, and the date that they were married, divorced, or separated.

    Example:

    Investment Gains and Losses

    • In order to have good financial planning you will need to know how to calculate how much percent you gain and lose.
      • Let say if your original deposit is 2,000 a month and it increases to 2,600. It shows you have added 600 more to your deposit. Divide 600 by the original bill which is 2,000 it will be equal to 0.3. This demonstrated you have gained 30%.

    Investment Recovery (asset or resource recovery)

    • The process of recouping the value of unused or end of life assets. Has three main elements: to identify, reuse, sell.
      • To sell a house, the person has to know what percentage increase is required for their home to be worth the $250,000 that they originally paid for it. The difference between the current value and the original value is still $55,000 but the beginning balance is $195,000. When $55,000 is divided by $195,000, the result is 0.28 or 28% increase.

    Weighted Average Investment Return

    • The product (or multiplication) of weights that are associated with different investment options and their respective returns and summing such weights to 100% or 1.
        Three investments comprise 50%, 30% and 20% of an investor’s portfolio and earn 7 percent, 5 percent and 3 percent respectively. The weighted average return would be 5.6 percent calculated as follows: (.07 x .50) + (.05 x .30)+ (.03 x .20) = 3.5 percent +1.5 percent +0.6 percent after decimal points are moved two places to the right.

    Net Worth

    • The benchmark of the growth in wealth by subtracting everything you owe from everything you own.
      • You have $600,000 including the house and your debt is $140,000 including the mortgage, so your net worth is $460,000.

    Cash Flow

    • To find out if your household expenses are positive or negative by subtracting.
      • If you earn 3500 from working and the money used on household expenses is 1200. Then you will have a positive cash flow of 2300. This helps you keep track if you use over expenses.

    Consumer Debt-to-Income Ratio

    • It is the percentage of the monthly debt payments, including the mortgage by your net income.
      • The payment of monthly debt is $300 with a net income of $3000, the ratio is $300 divided by $3000 or 10% .

    Break-Even Rate of Return

    • Involve the calculation and examination of the margin of safety (margin of safety - a principle of investing in which an investor only buys securities when their market price is significantly below their original value. In other words, when the market price of a security is significantly below your estimation of its original value, the difference is the margin of safety.
      • Return on all of your investments together that is needed to stay ahead of taxes and inflation. calculated by dividing an assumed inflation rate by 1 minus a person’s federal marginal tax bracket. For example, assuming a 4 percent average inflation rate over time, an investor in the 28 percent tax bracket would need to earn 5.6 percent → 4 /(1 - .28).

    Rule of 72

    • Tells you how long it will take to double your sum of any amount of money.
      • if you want to know an interest rated divide it with 72. ex: 72/6 (6% interest rate) = 12 yrs.

    Retirement Income Withdrawal Amount

    • Those who choose to retire should withdraw 4 percent of their investment.
      • A rule that helps to provide a steady income to the retiree while also maintaining an account balance that keeps income flowing through retirement.
